## Problem & motivation

The pure braid group is the fundamental group of the configuration space of
points in the plane — equivalently, the complement of the braid (type A)
hyperplane arrangement. A central question is how to read off a *presentation*
(generators and relations) of such groups directly from the combinatorics of
the underlying Coxeter arrangement.

## What I studied

I worked on presentations of pure braid groups via Coxeter arrangements, using
**galleries** (minimal paths between chambers) and the structure of **rank-2
subarrangements** as the source of the defining relations.

## Technical approach

- **Coxeter arrangements.** The reflecting hyperplanes of a finite Coxeter group
  cut space into chambers; loops in the complement encode the group.
- **Galleries.** Minimal galleries between chambers give canonical generators,
  and homotopies between galleries give relations.
- **Rank-2 reductions.** Relations localize to rank-2 subarrangements, so the
  global presentation is assembled from these local pieces.
