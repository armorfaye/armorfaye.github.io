## Problem & motivation

The pure braid group is the fundamental group of the configuration space of
points in the plane — equivalently, the complement of the type-A Coxeter
(hyperplane) arrangement. A classical result (Artin) gives a presentation by
generators and relations. The natural question: **can we generalize this to
arbitrary finite central real hyperplane arrangements?**

## What I studied

Working through MIT-PRIMES (Math Track) with Prof. Nathan Williams, I developed
a conjectural **full-twist presentation** for the fundamental group of the
complement of a complexified finite central real hyperplane arrangement.

## Technical approach

- **Salvetti complex.** A combinatorial model for the homotopy type of an
  arrangement complement, encoding chambers and face incidences.
- **Minimal galleries.** Generators arise from minimal galleries between chambers
  in the Salvetti complex.
- **Rank-2 subarrangements.** Relations are produced locally from rank-2
  subarrangements — the global presentation is assembled from these local pieces.
- **Full twist.** The central conjecture: the fundamental group is presented by
  equating all reduced words for the **full twist** element.

<Theorem label="Conjecture (Full-Twist Presentation)">
  The fundamental group of the complement of a complexified finite central real
  hyperplane arrangement admits a presentation whose defining relations are
  obtained by identifying all reduced words for the full twist, with generators
  corresponding to minimal galleries between chambers.
</Theorem>

<Theorem label="Verified cases" kind="proof">
  The conjecture is proved for Coxeter arrangements of types A, B, D, H₃,
  I₂(m), and partially for F₄. Each case reduces to checking that rank-2
  subarrangement relations generate all gallery homotopies.
</Theorem>

Symbolic computation (SageMath) was used to enumerate galleries and verify
candidate relations on small Coxeter types before attempting general proofs.
