## Problem & motivation

Alzheimer's disease affects millions worldwide, and early diagnosis enables better
care planning and clinical trial stratification. Standard CNN approaches on MRI
can classify severity but often miss **high-order topological structure** — how
connected components merge, how loops form around enlarged ventricles, how the
shape of intensity maps changes as tissue atrophies.

Topological data analysis (TDA) offers a complementary lens: instead of pixel
intensities alone, it measures the **shape** of the data across scales.

## What I built

An end-to-end pipeline on 461 OASIS MRI subjects (four severity classes:
Non-Demented, Very Mild, Mild, and Demented):

1. **Preprocessing** — 3D volumes sliced into ~60 axial 2D images per subject;
   intensity normalization to [0, 1].
2. **Topological feature extraction** — Vietoris–Rips filtrations on each slice;
   persistence diagrams aggregated into 20×20 persistence images (400-dim vectors).
3. **Classification** — random forest, multivariate logistic regression, and an
   AlexNet-style CNN trained with stratified 70/15/15 splits and 10-fold CV for
   hyperparameter tuning.

## Technical approach

- **Filtration.** 100 linearly spaced intensity thresholds; Ripser computes H₀
  (connected components) and H₁ (loops) persistence. Short-lived features (< 5
  voxel persistence) filtered as noise.
- **Persistence images.** Gaussian kernels (σ = 0.1) on a 20×20 birth–death grid,
  flattened to fixed-size vectors for classical ML.
- **CNN benchmark.** Three conv layers (32/64/128 filters), two FC layers, dropout
  0.5; slice-level predictions averaged per subject.

## Interpretability

Feature importance analysis showed the two most predictive variables were the
**maximum H₀ lifetime** and the **count of H₁ loops above a persistence threshold**
— consistent with ventricular enlargement (long-lived isolated components) and
cortical thinning (numerous topological voids) in AD progression.

## Limitations & outlook

The sample is modest and demographically limited (OASIS cohort). Future work includes
3D persistent homology on full volumes, hybrid CNN + topology models (our preliminary
hybrid slightly outperformed either alone), and validation on independent cohorts.
