## Problem & motivation

Falls are a leading cause of injury among older adults. Most monitoring systems
detect a fall **after impact** — too late to trigger a protective response. The
goal of TriFallNet is **pre-impact early warning**: enough lead time (~0.5s+) to
alert caregivers or activate protective measures before the person hits the ground.

## What I built

Two contributions:

1. **TriFallNet** — a transformer pipeline that takes video, estimates 3D pose,
   engineers kinematic features, and classifies motion windows as fall vs. activity
   of daily living (ADL) with an explicit early-warning objective.
2. **A unified fall-detection benchmark** — standardized preprocessing (25 Hz,
   256×256 RGB), harmonized labels (Fall vs. ADL), subject-disjoint splits across
   Le2i, URFD, and GMDCSA-24, and event-level metrics (lead time, event F1 under
   a fixed false-alarm budget).

## Technical approach

- **Preprocessing.** All clips resampled to 25 Hz, resized to 256×256 with
  aspect-ratio-preserving letterboxing.
- **3D pose.** MotionBERT-style estimator lifts 2D detections to 3D joint trajectories.
- **Kinematic features.** Center-of-mass velocity and torso inclination capture
  fall dynamics more directly than raw joints.
- **Skeleton-MixFormer.** A transformer fuses joint motion and kinematic features
  over time; a single decision threshold is chosen on validation and applied
  unchanged at test time.

## Joint importance

Analysis showed **lower-body joints** and **torso angle** are especially informative
for early detection — consistent with the biomechanics of losing balance.

## What's next

Generalize beyond falling to analyze joint importance across human actions, reduce
false alarms for clinical deployment, and expand to more diverse age groups and
real-world (non-simulated) datasets.
