## What this is

These notes are a structured walk through how modern LLMs are designed and
trained. The intent is to build deep, first-principles understanding and to
write it down clearly — not to claim original research. Sources are cited in the
notes themselves.

## Topics covered

- **Transformer internals.** Attention, normalization, positional schemes, and
  the trade-offs between architectural variants.
- **Mixture-of-experts (MoE).** Routing, capacity, load balancing, and why
  sparse models change the compute/quality curve.
- **Scaling laws.** How loss relates to parameters, data, and compute, and what
  that implies for allocating a fixed budget.
- **Distributed training.** Data, tensor, pipeline, and expert parallelism, plus
  the memory and communication bottlenecks behind each.
- **Midtraining.** Continued pretraining, data mixture shifts, and curriculum
  considerations between pretraining and post-training.
- **Optimization.** Optimizers, learning-rate schedules, and stability tricks at
  scale.
